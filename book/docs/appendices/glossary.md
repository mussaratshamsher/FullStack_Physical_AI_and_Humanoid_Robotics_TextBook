# Glossary

This appendix contains a glossary of key terms and concepts related to Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.

## A

**Actuator** - A device that converts energy (usually electrical) into mechanical motion. In humanoid robots, actuators drive joints and enable movement.

**Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)** - AI systems that possess human-like cognitive abilities across multiple domains, as opposed to narrow AI specialized in specific tasks.

**Autonomous Robot** - A robot capable of performing tasks without direct human intervention, using sensors and decision-making algorithms.

## B

**Bayesian Networks** - Probabilistic graphical models representing relationships between variables using directed acyclic graphs, commonly used in robotic perception and decision-making.

**Biomechanics** - The study of mechanical laws relating to the movement and structure of living organisms, essential for designing humanoid robots that mimic human movements.

**Bio-inspired Design** - Engineering approaches that replicate biological systems and structures to solve technological challenges.

## C

**Cognitive Architecture** - Blueprint for intelligent agent systems, defining structural elements and their relationships, including memory, perception, and reasoning capabilities.

**Computer Vision** - Field of AI focused on enabling machines to interpret and understand visual information from the world.

**Control Theory** - Branch of engineering dealing with dynamic systems and methods for influencing their behavior to achieve desired outcomes.

**Center of Mass (CoM)** - Point where total mass of a body is concentrated; crucial for balance and stability in humanoid robots.

## D

**Deep Learning** - Subset of machine learning using neural networks with multiple layers to model complex patterns in data.

**Degrees of Freedom (DOF)** - Number of independent movements a mechanical system can perform; important for measuring robot mobility.

**Dynamic Balance** - Ability to maintain equilibrium while moving, using continuous adjustment of body position and momentum.

## E

**Embodied AI** - Approach to artificial intelligence emphasizing that intelligent behavior emerges from the interaction between an agent and its environment.

**End Effector** - Device at the end of a robotic arm designed to interact with the environment, such as a gripper or tool.

**Entrainment** - Process by which rhythmic properties of one system become synchronized with those of another due to coupling between them.

## F

**Force Control** - Method of controlling a robot to apply a specific force to its environment, often used in manipulation tasks.

**Forward Kinematics** - Mathematical process of determining the position of a robot's end effector based on joint angles.

**Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM)** - 3D printing technique that creates objects by extruding thermoplastic material layer by layer.

## G

**Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM)** - Probabilistic model representing normally distributed subpopulations within an overall population.

**Generalized Coordinates** - Sets of coordinates that describe the configuration of a physical system, accounting for constraints.

**Gripper** - Mechanical device at the end of a robot arm for grasping objects.

## H

**Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)** - Study of interactions between humans and robots, focusing on design, evaluation, and implementation of robotic systems for human use.

**Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning** - Framework dividing complex tasks into subtasks arranged in a hierarchy, improving learning efficiency.

**Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL)** - Testing methodology combining real hardware with simulated environments to validate systems safely.

## I

**Inverse Kinematics** - Mathematical process of determining joint angles required to achieve a desired end-effector position.

**Imitation Learning** - Machine learning technique where an agent learns to perform tasks by mimicking expert demonstrations.

**Intention Recognition** - Process by which robots predict and understand human intentions based on observed behavior.

## J

**Joint Space** - Representation of robot configuration using joint angles instead of Cartesian coordinates.

**Jacobian Matrix** - Mathematical matrix describing relationship between joint velocities and end-effector velocities.

## K

**Kinematics** - Study of motion without considering forces causing it; includes forward and inverse kinematics.

**Kinesthetic Teaching** - Method of programming robots by physically guiding them through motions they should learn.

## L

**Legged Locomotion** - Method of movement using legs, presenting challenges in balance, coordination, and terrain adaptation.

**Lidar** - Sensor technology using laser light to measure distances and create 3D maps of environments.

**Locomotion Control** - Algorithms and mechanisms governing how robots move from one location to another.

## M

**Machine Learning** - Branch of AI enabling systems to learn and improve from experience without explicit programming.

**Manipulation** - Ability to handle and control objects using robot hands or end effectors.

**Motor Primitives** - Basic building blocks of motor control that can be combined to produce complex movements.

**Model Predictive Control (MPC)** - Control strategy using dynamic model to predict system behavior and optimize control inputs.

## N

**Neuromorphic Computing** - Computing architecture inspired by neural structure and function of the human brain.

**Natural Language Processing (NLP)** - Branch of AI focused on computer-human interaction through natural language.

## O

**Operational Space** - Coordinate system describing task space movements independently of joint space representation.

**Open Loop Control** - Control method without feedback, applying predetermined inputs regardless of output.

**Omnidirectional Wheels** - Wheels allowing movement in any direction without turning, useful for mobile robots.

## P

**Path Planning** - Algorithmic process of determining optimal route for robot to travel from start to goal.

**PID Controller** - Control loop mechanism using proportional, integral, and derivative terms to minimize error.

**Physically Grounded AI** - Approaches to AI requiring interaction with physical world to develop intelligence.

## R

**Reactive Robot** - Robot responding directly to current sensor inputs without maintaining internal state.

**Reinforcement Learning** - Machine learning paradigm where agents learn through trial and error with reward signals.

**Robot Operating System (ROS)** - Flexible framework for writing robot software, providing hardware abstraction and communication mechanisms.

**Rigid Body Dynamics** - Simulation of physics involving rigid bodies and their interactions.

## S

**Sensor Fusion** - Process of combining information from multiple sensors to obtain more accurate and reliable understanding.

**Servo Motor** - Rotary or linear actuator providing precise control of angular or linear position, velocity, and acceleration.

**Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM)** - Computational problem of constructing map while simultaneously keeping track of location.

**Social Robot** - Robot designed to interact with humans using social behaviors and cues.

## T

**Torque Control** - Method of controlling rotational force applied by robot joints, important for safe human-robot interaction.

**Trajectory Generation** - Process of computing smooth paths for robot movements with specified timing.

**Telerobotics** - Area of robotics controlling robots remotely from a distance.

## U

**Ubiquitous Robotics** - Integration of robotic services into everyday environments, making them readily accessible.

**Underactuated Systems** - Mechanical systems with fewer actuators than degrees of freedom, common in humanoid robots.

## V

**Visual Servoing** - Technique using visual feedback to control robot motion toward target positions.

**Virtual Reality (VR)** - Technology creating simulated environments for immersive experiences, sometimes used for robot teleoperation.

## W

**Whole-body Control** - Coordination of all robot degrees of freedom to achieve multiple simultaneous objectives.

**Walk Engine** - Control program generating walking patterns for legged robots.

**Workspace** - Volume in space where robot end effector can reach.
